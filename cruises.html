Plan Your Visit

Use this page as a quick checklist before going to a festival or international event in Japan.

Japan's major events can be busy, seasonal, and spread across several streets or venues. A little preparation makes the day much easier, especially for summer festivals, evening parades, and citywide event weekends.

  1. Check the exact date and venue before leaving. Festival routes and event spaces can change by year, weather, and crowd-control rules. Save the official event page, nearest station, and start time.

  2. Plan transportation early. Trains are usually the best choice for large events. Arrive before peak time, load your IC card, and choose a meeting point in case your group gets separated.

  3. Bring simple festival essentials. Comfortable shoes, water, a small towel, portable charger, and cash are helpful. Summer festivals can be hot, so shade breaks matter.

  4. Respect the event space. Follow staff directions, avoid blocking parade paths, and ask before photographing performers closely. A little patience goes a long way in crowded festival areas.
